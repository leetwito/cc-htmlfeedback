Orbit: Async Standups Without the Meeting

Product spec — please leave feedback inline. Last edited by J. Rivera · 3 comments unresolved.

Orbit replaces the daily synchronus standup with short written check-ins that your team posts on thier own schedule. Instead of blocking 15 minutes every morning, people answer three questions whenever they start work, and the digest lands in Slack at a time you choose. This document describes the v1 scope and the open questions we still need to resolve.

The core insight is simple: most standup updates are not urgent, and the ones that are urgent should not wait for a meeting anyway. By moving updates to asynchronous text, we reclaim roughly 3.75 hours per person per week and we create a searchable record that new teammates can actually read. At the end of the day, when all is said and done, the fundamental basic goal is to reduce meetings.

1. Problem

Synchronous standups force everyone into the same 15-minute window regardless of timezone, focus state, or whether they even have anything to report. For distributed teams this is especially painful: a 9:30am standup in New York is 6:30am in San Francisco and 2:30pm in London.

"We have engineers on four continents. The standup is convenient for exactly none of them, and the one person it does work for spends it reading their phone." — Anonymous EM, user interview

There is also a quality problem. Verbal updates evaporate. Nobody can search them, decisions made in standup go unrecorded, and anyone who was out sick has no way to catch up.

2. Proposed Solution

Orbit asks each person three questions, once per working day, at a time they configure:

  1. What did you finish since your last check-in?
  2. What are you working on next?
  3. Is anything blocking you? (Blockers get flagged to the lead immediately.)

2.1 Delivery

Responses are collected and posted as a single threaded digest to a channel of the team's choosing. Blockers are also DMed to the team lead in real time, so urgent items are not buried in the digest.

3. Technical Sketch

The bot is a thin Slack app backed by a scheduled worker. The prompt scheduler fans out per-user jobs based on each member's local check-in time; replies are stored and assembled into the digest. 

// pseudo: assemble the daily digest
const replies = await db.replies.forTeam(teamId, today);
const digest  = replies
  .sort((a, b) => a.user.localeCompare(b.user))
  .map(formatReply)
  .join("\n\n");
await slack.postMessage(team.digestChannel, digest);

⚠️ Open question: what happens to the digest if zero people have checked in by delivery time? Skip it silently, or post an empty-state nudge? We have not decided.

4. Open Questions

  • Should blockers escalate to anyone beyond the team lead after N hours unaddressed?
  • Do we support more than three questions, or is the constraint the point?
  • How do we handle people who are out of office without making them feel like they missed something?
